1. ICO PUBLISHES UPDATED AI GUIDANCE
   Domain: Data Protection
   Source: Information Commissioner's Office
   Reference: ICO-2026-0214-AI

   The ICO has published updated guidance on the use of AI and automated
   decision-making under UK GDPR Article 22. Key changes include:

   - Tighter definition of "meaningful human review" — rubber-stamping
     of automated decisions no longer sufficient
   - New risk-based framework for AI transparency obligations
   - Mandatory AI impact assessments for high-risk automated decisions
     affecting individuals
   - Enhanced requirements for explaining AI logic to data subjects

   Effective date: 1 March 2026
   Grace period: 3 months for existing AI systems (until 1 June 2026)
   Penalties: Existing UK GDPR penalties apply (up to £17.5m / 4% turnover)

   The ICO has confirmed AI enforcement as a priority for 2026-27.

2. CONSULTATION: REVISED BREACH NOTIFICATION THRESHOLDS
   Domain: Data Protection
   Source: Information Commissioner's Office
   Reference: ICO-CON-2026-003

   The ICO is consulting on proposals to revise the thresholds for
   mandatory data breach notification. The consultation considers:

   - Whether the current 72-hour notification window remains appropriate
   - Potential introduction of tiered notification based on breach severity
   - Simplified reporting for SMEs
   - Alignment with international breach notification standards

   Consultation closes: 30 April 2026
   Expected outcome: Autumn 2026

   All data controllers are encouraged to respond.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

3. HSE UPDATES WORKPLACE STRESS GUIDANCE
   Domain: Health & Safety
   Source: Health and Safety Executive
   Reference: HSE-2026-STRESS-UPDATE

   The HSE has updated its Management Standards approach to workplace
   stress, incorporating:

   - Explicit requirements for remote/hybrid working risk assessments
   - Updated guidance on psychosocial hazards including digital fatigue
   - New template tools for small businesses
   - Stronger emphasis on management training and early intervention

   Effective date: 1 April 2026
   No transition period — guidance applies immediately on publication.

   Note: While HSE Management Standards are guidance (not regulations),
   they are used as the benchmark in enforcement and tribunal cases.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

4. EMPLOYMENT RIGHTS BILL — COMMITTEE STAGE UPDATE
   Domain: Employment Law
   Source: UK Parliament
   Reference: ERB-2024-25-CS3

   The Employment Rights Bill has progressed through Commons Committee
   Stage with the following notable amendments:

   - Day-one unfair dismissal rights: statutory probationary period
     confirmed at 9 months
   - Zero-hours contracts: right to guaranteed hours after 12-week
     qualifying period
   - Fire and rehire: new statutory code with tribunal uplift of up to 25%
   - Trade union access: digital access rights to workplace for organising

   Expected Royal Assent: Summer 2026
   Most provisions expected to take effect: October 2026 or April 2027

   Employers should begin planning for significant changes to
   employment practices.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────

5. FCA CONSUMER DUTY — 12-MONTH REVIEW FINDINGS
   Domain: Financial Services
   Source: Financial Conduct Authority
   Reference: FCA-CD-REVIEW-2026

   The FCA has published findings from its 12-month review of the
   Consumer Duty implementation. Key findings:

   - Price and value assessments: many firms need to improve methodology
   - Vulnerability identification: good progress but inconsistent
   - Board reporting: needs to move beyond tick-box compliance
   - Closed products: reminder that July 2026 deadline for closed
     products/services is approaching

   Closed products deadline: 31 July 2026
   No new regulations — but signals enforcement focus areas.
