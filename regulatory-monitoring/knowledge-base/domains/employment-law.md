# Employment Law — Regulatory Baseline

## Core Legislation

### Employment Rights Act 1996
- Unfair dismissal protection (2 years' qualifying service)
- Redundancy rights and consultation requirements
- Written statement of employment particulars (day-one right)
- Flexible working requests (day-one right from April 2024)
- Protection from detriment for whistleblowing

### Equality Act 2010
- Protected characteristics: age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, sexual orientation
- Direct and indirect discrimination
- Harassment and victimisation
- Equal pay
- Reasonable adjustments for disabled workers
- Positive action provisions

### Working Time Regulations 1998
- Maximum 48-hour working week (opt-out available)
- Minimum 5.6 weeks paid annual leave (28 days for full-time)
- Rest breaks: 20 minutes if working 6+ hours
- Daily rest: 11 consecutive hours
- Weekly rest: 24 hours uninterrupted (or 48 hours per fortnight)

### National Minimum Wage Act 1998
- National Living Wage (21+): rates updated annually in April
- Age-specific bands: 18-20, under 18, apprentice rate
- Employers must keep records for 3 years

### Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974
- See health-safety.md for detailed baseline

## Family-Related Rights
- Maternity leave: 52 weeks (39 weeks paid)
- Paternity leave: 2 weeks
- Shared parental leave: up to 50 weeks
- Parental leave: 18 weeks unpaid per child
- Adoption leave: mirrors maternity provisions
- Carer's leave: 1 week unpaid (from April 2024)
- Neonatal care leave: up to 12 weeks (from April 2025)

## TUPE (Transfer of Undertakings)
- Employee rights transfer with business/service transfers
- Obligation to inform and consult representatives
- Protection against dismissal connected to transfer

## Trade Union and Industrial Relations
- Right to join a trade union
- Statutory recognition procedure
- Collective bargaining obligations
- Industrial action balloting requirements

## Immigration and Right to Work
- Right to work checks mandatory before employment starts
- Digital right to work checks via Home Office online service
- Penalties for employing illegal workers: up to £45,000 per worker (first offence)

## Key Regulators
- **Employment Tribunals** — individual disputes
- **ACAS** — conciliation and guidance
- **HMRC** — national minimum wage enforcement
- **Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC)** — equality law enforcement
- **Gangmasters and Labour Abuse Authority (GLAA)** — labour exploitation

## Penalties
- Unfair dismissal: compensatory award capped (updated annually, currently ~£115,000)
- Discrimination: uncapped compensation
- NMW non-compliance: 200% of arrears + naming and shaming
- Right to work failures: up to £45,000 per worker
