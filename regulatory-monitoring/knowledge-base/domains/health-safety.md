# Health & Safety — Regulatory Baseline

## Core Legislation

### Health and Safety at Work etc. Act 1974 (HSWA)
- General duty on employers to ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health, safety, and welfare of employees
- Duty to non-employees (visitors, contractors, public) affected by the undertaking
- Duty on employees to take reasonable care for themselves and others
- Requirement for a written health and safety policy (5+ employees)
- Duty to consult employees on health and safety matters

### Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
- Risk assessment obligation (written if 5+ employees)
- Competent person(s) to assist with health and safety
- Emergency procedures
- Health surveillance where appropriate
- Information for employees
- Co-operation between employers sharing a workplace

### Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations 1992
- Temperature, ventilation, lighting
- Cleanliness and waste
- Room dimensions and space
- Workstations and seating
- Floors, traffic routes
- Sanitary conveniences, washing facilities
- Rest areas and eating facilities

### COSHH (Control of Substances Hazardous to Health) 2002
- Assessment of hazardous substance exposure
- Prevention or control of exposure
- Monitoring, health surveillance
- Information, instruction, and training

### Display Screen Equipment Regulations 1992
- Workstation assessments for habitual users
- Eye tests and corrective appliances
- Breaks and activity changes
- Training and information

### RIDDOR (Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences) 2013
- Report deaths, specified injuries, over-7-day incapacitation
- Report occupational diseases
- Report dangerous occurrences (near misses)
- Report to HSE online or by phone (fatalities)
- Keep records for 3 years

### Fire Safety (Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005)
- Fire risk assessment
- Fire detection and warning systems
- Means of escape
- Fire-fighting equipment
- Staff training
- Fire safety plan and review

## Key Duties by Role
- **Responsible person**: overall accountability
- **Competent person**: day-to-day health and safety management
- **Safety representatives**: employee consultation
- **First aiders**: adequate first-aid provision

## Key Regulators
- **Health and Safety Executive (HSE)** — primary enforcer for most workplaces
- **Local authorities** — enforce in offices, shops, hotels, catering, leisure
- **Fire and rescue services** — fire safety enforcement

## Penalties
- **Improvement notice**: requirement to fix issues within a timeframe
- **Prohibition notice**: immediate cessation of dangerous activity
- **Criminal prosecution**:
  - Unlimited fines for organisations
  - Individuals: unlimited fines and/or up to 2 years' imprisonment
- **Corporate manslaughter**: unlimited fine + publicity order + remedial order
- **Sentencing guidelines**: based on culpability, harm, and turnover

## HSE Enforcement Priorities
- Work at height
- Asbestos management
- Workplace transport
- Musculoskeletal disorders
- Stress and mental health
- Construction (CDM Regulations 2015)
