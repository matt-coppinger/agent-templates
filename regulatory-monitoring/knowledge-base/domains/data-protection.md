# Data Protection — Regulatory Baseline

## UK GDPR & Data Protection Act 2018

### Key Principles (Article 5)
1. **Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency** — personal data must be processed lawfully and transparently
2. **Purpose limitation** — collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
3. **Data minimisation** — adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary
4. **Accuracy** — kept accurate and up to date
5. **Storage limitation** — kept no longer than necessary
6. **Integrity and confidentiality** — appropriate security measures
7. **Accountability** — controller must demonstrate compliance

### Lawful Bases for Processing (Article 6)
- Consent
- Contract
- Legal obligation
- Vital interests
- Public task
- Legitimate interests (requires balancing test)

### Individual Rights
- Right of access (Subject Access Requests — 1 month response time)
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object
- Rights related to automated decision-making (Article 22)

### Data Breach Notification
- Notify ICO within **72 hours** of becoming aware of a breach (if risk to individuals)
- Notify affected individuals **without undue delay** if high risk
- Maintain a breach register regardless of notification

### Data Protection Officer (DPO)
Required for:
- Public authorities
- Large-scale systematic monitoring
- Large-scale processing of special category data

### International Transfers
- Adequacy decisions (post-Brexit: UK maintains own adequacy list)
- Standard Contractual Clauses (UK International Data Transfer Agreement)
- Binding Corporate Rules

### Penalties
- **Tier 1**: up to £8.7m or 2% of annual global turnover
- **Tier 2**: up to £17.5m or 4% of annual global turnover

### PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003)
- Direct marketing rules (opt-in for electronic marketing)
- Cookie consent requirements
- Confidentiality of communications

## Key Regulators
- **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)** — primary data protection regulator
- **Ofcom** — communications-related privacy

## Current Enforcement Priorities (2025-26)
- AI and automated decision-making
- Children's data and age-appropriate design
- Adtech and real-time bidding
- Public sector data handling
