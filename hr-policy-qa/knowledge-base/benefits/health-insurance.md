# Health Insurance Benefit

**Policy reference:** HR-BEN-002
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Review date:** 1 January 2027

## Overview

The company provides private medical insurance (PMI) to all permanent employees who have completed their probationary period (typically 3 months).

## Provider

The company scheme is provided by **Bupa**, with cover under the Corporate Select plan.

## What's Covered

- GP consultations and specialist referrals
- Diagnostic tests (MRI, CT scans, blood tests)
- Inpatient and day-patient treatment
- Outpatient surgery and consultations
- Cancer treatment
- Mental health support (up to £1,500 per year)
- Physiotherapy (up to 10 sessions per condition)

## What's Not Covered

- Pre-existing conditions (in the first year of cover)
- Dental and optical treatment (separate scheme available — see below)
- Chronic condition management (ongoing, non-curative treatment)
- Cosmetic surgery
- Pregnancy and childbirth (covered by the NHS)

## Family Cover

- **Employee:** covered at no additional cost
- **Partner:** available at a subsidised rate of £45/month (deducted from salary)
- **Children:** available at £25/month per child (up to 4 children)

Family cover can be added during the annual enrolment window (November) or within 30 days of a qualifying life event (marriage, birth, adoption).

## How to Use

1. Register on the Bupa Members portal at [bupa.co.uk/members](https://www.bupa.co.uk/members)
2. Your company scheme number is **BUP-CORP-7291**
3. For treatment, call Bupa on **0345 600 1020** to obtain pre-authorisation
4. Choose a Bupa-recognised consultant or hospital

Pre-authorisation is required before any treatment. Claims without pre-authorisation may not be reimbursed.

## Dental & Optical

A separate dental and optical cash plan is available through **Simplyhealth** at £12/month (employee contribution). This covers:
- 2 dental check-ups per year (up to £100 each)
- 1 eye test per year (up to £50)
- Contribution towards glasses/contact lenses (up to £150 every 2 years)

## Benefit in Kind

Private medical insurance is a taxable benefit in kind. The value will be reported on your P11D and you will pay tax on it through your tax code.
