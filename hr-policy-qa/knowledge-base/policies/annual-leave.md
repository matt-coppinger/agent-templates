# Annual Leave Policy

**Policy reference:** HR-POL-001
**Effective date:** 1 January 2026
**Review date:** 1 January 2027

## Statutory Entitlement

Under the Working Time Regulations 1998, all workers are entitled to a minimum of 5.6 weeks' paid annual leave per year (28 days for full-time employees, inclusive of bank holidays).

## Company Entitlement

| Length of Service | Annual Leave (excl. bank holidays) | Total (incl. 8 bank holidays) |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 years | 25 days | 33 days |
| 2–5 years | 27 days | 35 days |
| 5+ years | 30 days | 38 days |

Part-time employees receive a pro-rata entitlement based on their contracted hours.

## Holiday Year

The holiday year runs from 1 January to 31 December.

## Booking Leave

- Leave requests must be submitted via the HR system at least 2 weeks in advance
- Requests of 5+ consecutive days require 4 weeks' notice
- Line manager approval is required for all leave requests
- The company may decline requests during peak business periods (November–December)

## Carryover

- Employees may carry over up to **5 days** of unused annual leave to the following holiday year
- Carryover requires line manager approval before 15 December
- Carried-over days must be taken by **31 March** of the following year
- Untaken carried-over days are forfeited after 31 March
- Different rules apply for employees returning from maternity, paternity, adoption, or long-term sick leave — contact HR for details

## Payment in Lieu

Annual leave cannot be exchanged for payment except upon termination of employment. On leaving, employees are entitled to payment for accrued but untaken leave, or may be required to repay leave taken in excess of accrual.

## Unauthorised Absence

Failure to follow the leave booking procedure may result in absence being recorded as unauthorised, which may lead to disciplinary action.
