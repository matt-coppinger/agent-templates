# Standard Warranties and Representations

Standard warranty and representation clauses commonly sought in UK share purchase agreements. Use as a reference when recommending contractual protections.

## 1. Title and Capacity
- The seller has full title to the shares, free from encumbrances
- The seller has the authority and capacity to enter into the transaction
- No consents or approvals are required (or all have been obtained)

## 2. Accounts Warranties
- The accounts have been prepared in accordance with applicable accounting standards and give a true and fair view
- The accounts are not misleading and do not materially overstate assets or understate liabilities
- Since the accounts date, the business has been carried on in the ordinary course
- No material adverse change since the accounts date

## 3. Tax Warranties
- All tax returns have been properly submitted on time
- All tax liabilities have been paid or adequately provided for
- There are no outstanding HMRC enquiries or disputes
- No transaction has been entered into for the purpose of avoiding tax
- The company is not party to any tax sharing or group relief arrangements that create ongoing exposure

## 4. Contracts and Commitments
- A complete list of material contracts has been disclosed
- All material contracts are valid, binding, and in full force
- No material contract is subject to a dispute or termination notice
- No material contract contains change of control provisions (or all have been disclosed)
- The company is not in breach of any material contract

## 5. Litigation and Disputes
- There is no pending or threatened litigation, arbitration, or regulatory proceeding
- There are no circumstances likely to give rise to such proceedings
- All historical litigation has been disclosed

## 6. Employees
- A complete list of employees with terms has been disclosed
- There are no pending or threatened employment claims
- The company is compliant with all employment legislation
- All pension obligations are fully funded and disclosed
- TUPE has been properly considered and all obligations will be met

## 7. Intellectual Property
- The company owns or has valid licences for all IP used in the business
- No IP is subject to any third-party claims or disputes
- All registrable IP has been properly registered and maintained
- No employee or contractor IP assignment is outstanding
- The company's products and services do not infringe third-party IP

## 8. Real Property
- The company has good title to all freehold properties
- All leases are valid and the company is not in breach
- There are no outstanding disputes with landlords or neighbours
- All necessary planning permissions and building regulations have been obtained
- No environmental contamination or remediation obligations exist

## 9. Compliance and Regulatory
- The company holds all necessary licences, permits, and authorisations
- The company is in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
- There are no pending regulatory investigations or proceedings
- The company is compliant with data protection legislation (GDPR)
- Adequate anti-bribery and anti-money laundering procedures are in place

## 10. Insurance
- Adequate insurance is in place for the business and its assets
- All policies are current and premiums paid
- There are no outstanding or threatened claims
- No circumstances exist that might give rise to a claim

## 11. Information and Disclosure
- All information provided in the data room is true, complete, and not misleading
- The disclosure letter is complete and accurate
- No material facts have been withheld
