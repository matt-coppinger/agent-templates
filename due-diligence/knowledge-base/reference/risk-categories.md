# Risk Categories for Due Diligence

## 1. Litigation Risk
Pending, threatened, or historical legal claims that could result in financial liability or operational disruption.

**Indicators:**
- Active court proceedings or tribunal claims
- Solicitor correspondence threatening action
- Historical pattern of claims in the same area
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Class action exposure

**Typical impact:** Direct financial liability, legal costs, management distraction, reputational damage.

## 2. Regulatory Risk
Non-compliance with applicable laws and regulations, or exposure to regulatory change.

**Indicators:**
- Missing or expired licences and permits
- Regulatory correspondence indicating concerns
- Non-compliance with GDPR, health and safety, environmental regulations
- Industry-specific regulatory changes pending
- Anti-bribery or sanctions exposure

**Typical impact:** Fines, licence revocation, enforced operational changes, criminal liability.

## 3. Contract Dependency Risk
Over-reliance on specific contracts or relationships that could be disrupted by the transaction.

**Indicators:**
- Change of control clauses in material contracts
- Key contracts approaching expiry without renewal certainty
- Single-source supplier dependencies
- Customer concentration (>15% revenue from one customer)
- Contracts with onerous termination provisions

**Typical impact:** Revenue loss, supply chain disruption, increased costs.

## 4. Intellectual Property Risk
Weaknesses in IP protection or ownership that could affect the value of the business.

**Indicators:**
- Unregistered trademarks or patents
- IP created by contractors without proper assignment
- Third-party infringement claims
- Expiring patent protection on key products
- Open-source licence compliance issues
- Trade secrets without adequate protection measures

**Typical impact:** Loss of competitive advantage, infringement liability, inability to enforce rights.

## 5. Employee and HR Risk
Liabilities or risks arising from the workforce.

**Indicators:**
- Pending employment tribunal claims
- Defined benefit pension scheme deficits
- Key person dependency without succession planning
- TUPE transfer complications
- Restrictive covenant gaps for senior employees
- Equal pay or discrimination exposure
- Collective bargaining agreements

**Typical impact:** Financial liabilities, loss of key staff, operational disruption, tribunal costs.

## 6. Environmental Risk
Environmental liabilities or compliance obligations.

**Indicators:**
- Land contamination (current or historical)
- Environmental permits with onerous conditions
- Asbestos presence in properties
- Carbon emission obligations
- Waste management compliance
- Environmental prosecution history

**Typical impact:** Remediation costs, regulatory fines, operational restrictions, reputational damage.

## 7. Financial Risk
Financial exposures not fully reflected in the accounts.

**Indicators:**
- Contingent liabilities not provisioned
- Off-balance-sheet arrangements
- Tax disputes or uncertain tax positions
- Going concern qualifications in audit reports
- Related party transactions at non-market rates
- Debt covenants at risk of breach

**Typical impact:** Unexpected financial liabilities, price adjustment requirements.

## 8. Commercial Risk
Risks to the ongoing commercial viability of the business.

**Indicators:**
- Declining revenue trends
- Market disruption or technology obsolescence
- Customer churn above industry norms
- Margin compression
- Competitive threats
- Regulatory changes affecting the market

**Typical impact:** Reduced future earnings, lower valuation, strategic risk.
